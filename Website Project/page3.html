PROJECT POSTMORTEM

Yeah, don't be surprised at such an empty page. This here is the Postmortem!

You know, when I first started HTML, I had not a clue as to what I was doing: I used templates given by the Professor, and kind of just... went wild.

I never really saw the potential that HTML had; matter-of-fact, I never really wanted to learn it. "I want to make games," I said, "not webpages."

And, I mean, for the most part? I was right. I still do want to make video games, but with the completion of this project, I've come to realize that HTML isn't just a service:

It's art.

When I was looking up YouTube guides and researching directories through various sources (including AI, which was NOT used in the writing of code), it all looked complicated, but with a little bit of help, I managed to get things running. Now, I've made film grains and vignettes on my pages, and I even made a typewriting animation! (How cool is that?)

I think what I'm trying to say is that I never thought I would make it as far as I have now, and it's all thanks to my college and my professor for getting me into the craft. I actually want to make websites now!

Thus, I made this page less to say, "I'm proud of what I made," and more to say thank you. Thank you, thank you, thank you for showing me what HTML could really do.

Off to the next language... in whatever class I have next quarter! (Time to check the schedule again.. T^T)
